Work on A2 plan document, specifically testing strategy section. Researched methods of formally verifying cryptographic code, discovered ProVerif and Tamarin. Thinking about attack vectors, and what attackers may try to do. Not all attacks will be against the cryptography which must be carefully considered!
One attack I need to investigate is the length attack, where a redacted section of text could be guessed (but not verified) due to its length (along with context related to the message). Similar (in philosophy) attacks have recently been used to uncover redacted names in the epstein files, though that particular example uses variable width fonts and related analysis such as pixel artefacts to guess at unknown text.
Even a completely redacted piece of text leaks information via its length:
"masked_text_utf8": "***************",
For example, a yes/no answer could be leaked via the redaction being two or three characters.

1-4pm 19/03
More literature review. Found new keyword: "redactable signature scheme" (RSS)

All of the papers below are about taking some message, and using a specific known scheme to redact part of it in some way, with novel contributions to only allowing redaction of part of the message, or about the kinds of messages that can be redacted or how long it takes to redact.
We are interested in redacting existing signed messages without the authors consent or knowledge necessary, through ZKPs. They are alternative technologies. I will need to find more papers focused on our area.

https://eprint.iacr.org/2015/1059.pdf
General framework for redacting messages without regard for framework or data structure. Mainly goes over mathematical constraints that must be satisfied and proves that if they are then it works. Could be used as a basis for proving our system?
https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2022arXiv220209807T/abstract
Allows redaction only once
https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/1579.pdf
For redacting images, has novel ideas due to the time complexity of large images being redacted

https://www.iso.org/standard/78342.html
ISO standard about redacting data. "allow the attestation to remain verifiable using the verification transformation and attest that non-redacted fields of the attested message are unmodified."

https://arxiv.org/pdf/2202.09799
Has a lot of math stuff. Does the technique by committing to a set. This addresses some issues with length, i.e. signature has linear signature size to number of elements in message. This architecture is a signing technique though, so it requires the message to be signed in this way.

https://eprint.iacr.org/2002/016
First proposed in 2002 under the name "Content Extraction Signature"

https://scholarworks.merrimack.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1064&context=cs_facpub
Novel contribution of the author allowing only certain parts of the text to be redacted

------ZKPs------

https://arxiv.org/pdf/2310.15934
Notes the faster speed of ZKPs particularly using Groth16 SNARKs over other technologies. However, they raise concerns about the requirements of setting up the proof which can be somewhat mitigated with our project.

https://eprint.iacr.org/2022/878.pdf
zk-Creds, a system for identity proving using zkSnarks

https://www.eurecom.fr/publication/5311/download/sec-publi-5311.pdf
Basically our idea.
They create a link between the original document and the redacted one using a set of places where the redaction occurred, where the redacted document only differs in places defined by that set.
They also do a weird step where the document issuer appends a random value to the document to make the hash un-usable. I'm not sure why this is needed?
